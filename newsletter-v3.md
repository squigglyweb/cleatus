## A Little History

The name "Waxhaw" comes from the Waxhaw people — an indigenous tribe that lived here long before anyone put us on a map. They were related to the Catawba, and they had a custom that's hard to forget.

They practiced artificial cranial deformation. At birth, they'd bind a baby's head to a flat board to reshape their skull. The wider eyes that resulted were said to give them better vision for hunting.

It's the kind of history that makes you realize — this land has seen some things.

The tribe is gone now, absorbed and displaced like so many others. But the name stuck. That's part of what makes this place feel different. We've got roots that go deeper than the shopping centers on Providence Road would have you believe.

## The Essentials

Everyone needs a few go-to people. Here are the ones our neighbors won't shut up about:

**Your baker**: Matthews Cafe. Been here forever. The biscuits are the kind your grandmother would lie about, and the apple fritters sell out fast. Get there early on weekends.

**Your mechanic**: Cox Automotive on Rosewood. Old-school honest. They'll tell you what's actually wrong, not what sounds expensive.

**Your coffee person**: Daylight Coffee downtown. Small, always busy, but they remember your name after a few visits. The cold brew is worth the wait.

## Your Weekend Sorted

**Saturday morning**: Waxhaw Farmer's Market. Yes, we have one. Yes, it's actually good. Grab coffee, walk around, let the kids pick out something they'll refuse to eat later. It's a ritual.

**Saturday afternoon**: Backup plans for "we're bored":
- **Crafty Kids** in Marvin — painting ceramics, no appointment needed. They'll hand your kid a brush and a dinosaur and suddenly you've got 45 minutes of peace.
- **Carmel Kitchen** — half-day camp for kids, real food for you. Win-win.
- **Lake Lloyd at Cane Creek Park** — fishing, playground, enough space to wear them out. Pack sunscreen.

**Friday night**: Date night. Keep reading.

## Date Night (Full Guide on the Blog)

*The full guide is on our blog this week — here's the quick list:*

**The splurge**: Kitchen | San José. Rooftop bar, good empanadas, worth the drive from anywhere in the area.

**The romantic one**: The Mill. Old textile mill, farm-to-table, reservations required (book two weeks out).

**The hidden gem**: Talley's BBQ. Gas station exterior, legitimately incredible smoked meat. Don't overthink it — just go.

**The easy night**: Waxhaw Brewing Company. Local beer, live music on weekends, dog-friendly. Low pressure, high reward.

**The "we need to actually talk" place**: 4000 Mexican Grill. It's loud enough to have a real conversation, the margaritas are strong, and the guac is made fresh at your table.

## Holidays Coming Up

**Memorial Day (May 26)**: Cane Creek Park does a community cookout. Not fancy. That's the point. Bring a side, stay for the fireworks.

**Fourth of July**: The parade on Providence Road is the highlight of the year. Get there by 9am, stake your spot on the curb, bring lawn chairs. The fireworks launch from the high school field — any hill on the north side works if you want to skip the crowd.

**Back to School**: The Teacher Supply Store on Highway 16 does a backpack drive. We'll remind you closer to time. Most people miss it.

## Why We're Here

Local isn't a vibe. It's math.

Every dollar you spend at a local business stays in this community — wages for your neighbor, support for the school fundraiser, the lights on at the body shop that's been there since before your neighborhood existed.

Here's the part that matters to us: **every business on NearBuy helps feed families.** Right here in Waxhaw and around the world. We call it Glocal: local heart, global reach.

When you choose local, you're already making a difference. We're just making sure you know it.
