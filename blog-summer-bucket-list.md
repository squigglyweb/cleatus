# The Summer Bucket List: 50 Things to Do in Greater Waxhaw Before School Starts

**Your survival guide for the next 75 days.**

---

School's out in (counting). You have roughly seventy-five days to fill before the routine kicks back in and you start Googling "how to make a calendar desk again." (Just us?)

Here's the thing: there's actually stuff to do here. You've just never had a list.

This is that list. Fifty things. Some are obvious, some are secrets, all of them are better than screen time. Let's go.

---

## 🌊 Water & Cooling Off

1. **Cane Creek Park** — Lake Lloyd has a swimming area, fishing pier, and playground. Lifeguards on duty in the summer. Get there by 10am to claim the good spot.

2. **The Splash Pad at the Waxhaw Town Park** — Free, no fuss, good for toddlers through elementary. Bring towels and a change of clothes. There's a pavilion if you need shade.

3. **Stewart's Creek (Secret swimming hole)** — Google "Stewart's Creek access Waxhaw." There's a dirt pull-off where locals park. Rocky bottom, tree swing, water deep enough to float. Go early. Bring bug spray.

4. **Catawba River access** — Multiple put-ins between Waxhaw and Lancaster. Kayak or tube downstream. The current's gentle, the scenery isn't.

5. **Lake Twitty** — In Marvin. Less crowded than Cane Creek, still has a beach. Kayak rentals available on-site.

6. **Monkey Joe's** — Indian Land. It's a bounce house place. You're going to take them eventually. Get the unlimited pass, stay for two hours, let them wear themselves out.

---

## 🏃 Active & Outdoors

7. **Cane Creek Greenway** — Paved path, 3+ miles, stroller-friendly. Start at the park and go until you hit the bridge. Bring water.

8. **Marvin Loop** — The trail behind Marvin Elementary connects to the neighborhood paths. Great for a family bike ride.

9. **Waxhaw Soccer Club fields** — Open to the public when there's no practice. Big open grass, bring a kite or a frisbee.

10. **The Green at Wesley Chapel** — Newer park, great playground equipment, paved paths. Good for younger kids.

11. **Nations Ford Nature Preserve** — Short hike, creek access, good for kids who like exploring. In the Indian Land area.

12. **Piedmont Natural Gas Trail** — Part of the greenway system. Connects to longer routes if you're feeling ambitious.

---

## 🎨 Arts, Crafts & Making Things

13. **Crafty Kids** — Marvin. Painting ceramics. No appointment needed most days. They'll hand your kid a brush and a dinosaur and suddenly you've got forty-five minutes of peace. $10-20 per kid.

14. **Pottery Studio at The Spring fn** — Indian Land. Drop-in wheel throwing sessions. Not cheap ($40+) but genuinely cool. Teens love it.

15. **Waxhaw Makers Market** — First Saturday of each month downtown. Local crafts, food trucks, live music. Great for walking around.

16. **The Painted Pineapple** — Waxhaw. Paint-your-own pottery, but also sip-and-paint nights. Book ahead for weekends.

---

## 🎢 Festivals & Events

17. **Waxhaw Summerfest** — Usually late June. Parade, live music, food vendors, carnival rides on the field behind the high school. Free entry.

18. **Fourth of July Parade** — Providence Road. Get there by 9am to stake your curb spot. Bring lawn chairs. The fireworks launch from the high school football field — any hill on the north side gives you a view without the crowd.

19. **Marvin Day** — Usually September. Car show, live music, kids zone. Smaller than Summerfest but worth it.

20. **Movies in the Park** — Check the Waxhaw town events calendar. Some summer evenings they show free movies at the park after dark.

21. **Free concerts at Wesley Chapel Park** — Some Friday nights in summer. Bring a blanket, grab takeout, make a night of it.

---

## 🍦 Food & Treats

22. **Matthews Cafe** — The biscuits alone are worth the trip. Get there early, get the apple fritter, thank me later.

23. **Daylight Coffee** — Small, always busy, worth it. Cold brew is the move. They know orders after a few visits.

24. **Talley's BBQ** — Gas station exterior, incredible smoked meat inside. Get the brisket. Get extra sauce.

25. **Bruster's** — Indian Land. Real ice cream, local chain. The buttercream chocolate is the one.

26. **The Baked Bear** — Ballantyne area. Custom ice cream sandwiches. Yes, it's worth the drive.

27. **Krispy Kreme** — Yeah, it's a chain. But the one on Highway 16 is open 24/7 and has the heat shop right there. Late night runs are a tradition here.

28. **Waxhaw Farmer's Market** — Saturday mornings. Local produce, honey, baked goods, plants. Good for getting the kids involved.

---

## 🎳 Entertainment & Fun

29. **Strike! Entertainment** — Indian Land. Bowling, arcade, pizza. Not cheap but they have deals.

30. **Tiger Strikes** — Charlotte. Duckpin bowling, which is different enough to be interesting. About 20 minutes away.

31. **Boxcar** — Free Morehead City. Arcade, pinball, drinks. More for adults but teens can get in.

32. **Discovery Place Kids** — Huntersville. Science museum, water play area, big enough to spend half a day. About 25 minutes.

33. **Great Wolf Lodge** — Concord. Indoor water park. Day passes available. Expensive but it's the big summer treat.

34. **Sky Zone** — Charlotte. Trampoline park. Kids burn energy, you sit and watch.

35. **The蛤蚧 Movies** — TicketSaver in Monroe is the closest theater. Or drive to Ballantyne for more options.

---

## 🎓 Learning & Exploring

36. **Waxhaw Historical Museum** — Downtown. Small but worth a visit. Learn about the textile mill, the tribe, the railroad.

37. **Catawba Cultural Center** — Rock Hill (about 25 min). Native American history, pottery, demonstrations. Good for teaching kids.

38. **REI Family Hiking Day** — They do free guided hikes in the area. Check their calendar.

39. **Public Library** — The Waxhaw branch on Highway 16. Summer reading program, events, free. AC is free too.

40. **Farm visits** — There are u-pick farms in the area. Black's Blueberry Farm in Lancaster. Seasonal, call ahead.

---

## 🛍️ Shopping & Browsing

41. **Downtown Waxhaw** — Antiques, boutique clothing, home goods. One-of-a-kind shops. Park by the mill and walk.

42. **The Mill** — Part shop, part home goods, part café. Great for finding unique gifts.

43. **Marvin Exchange** — Old shopping center, new energy. Good local spots mixed in with the usual suspects.

44. **Indian Land antique shops** — Highway 521 toward Lancaster. Several in a row. Worth the drive if you dig.

45. **Wesley Chapel Farmers Market** — Sundays. Smaller than Waxhaw's but good produce.

---

## 🍽️ Date Night / Parent's Night Out

46. **Kitchen | San José** — Rooftop drinks, good food. Make a reservation.

47. **The Mill** — Romantic, farm-to-table. Book two weeks out.

48. **Waxhaw Brewing Company** — Live music, local beer, dog-friendly. Low key.

49. **4000 Mexican Grill** — Loud enough to talk, good enough to justify the drive.

50. **Talley's BBQ** — The secret weapon. Trust me on this.

## The Secret to Summer

Here's the thing: you're going to need backup plans. The weather will break your plans. The kids will say "I'm bored" at 2pm on a Tuesday. You'll need somewhere to go.

That's why we wrote this list.

Print it. Save it. Lose it in the diaper bag and find it in August. You'll thank yourself.
