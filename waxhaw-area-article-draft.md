## 🔥 Burn Ban Alert (As of Late March 2026)

**Yes — the burn ban is STILL in effect.**

Both Union County and the Town of Waxhaw issued burn bans in late March 2026 due to dry conditions. The ban prohibits all outdoor burning within 100 feet of an occupied home — including fire pits, burn barrels, campfires, and yard debris burning.

**What you CAN still use:**
- Gas grills
- Charcoal grills
- Smokers and cooking appliances

**Violations:** Up to $200 per occurrence plus potential state fines.

The ban remains in effect until the NC Forest Service lifts the statewide ban OR local fire marshals determine there's been sufficient rainfall. [Report open burning immediately by dialing 911.](https://www.unioncountync.gov/government/departments-a-e/emergency-services/fire-marshal-s-office/burning-regulations)

## 📜 A Quick History of Waxhaw

**The name "Waxhaw" comes from the indigenous Native American tribe** that lived in the area before European settlement.

**Key historical highlights:**

- **1711:** The Waxhaw tribe aided British colonists in the Tuscarora War, which led to raids and eventually their destruction in the Yamasee War (1715), freeing the land for European settlement

- **Mid-1700s:** German and Scots-Irish settlers arrived as subsistence farmers

- **1767:** **Andrew Jackson** was born nearby (the Waxhaws region straddles the NC/SC border). There are historical markers around Waxhaw documenting his early connection to the area. The Museum of the Waxhaws and Andrew Jackson State Park are minutes from downtown.

- **Early 1800s:** Gold was first discovered in the United States in the Piedmont region near Waxhaw. The Howie Gold Mine produced 50,000 ounces of gold by 1935 and operated until WWII.

- **1888:** The railroad arrived, bringing prosperity and access to Atlanta markets. The tracks were laid through the center of town — and they're still there today, now bordered by landscaped areas.

- **Late 1800s-1940s:** Cotton mill factories drove the local economy

- **Today:** Waxhaw has evolved into an **antique and fine dining center** with a historic downtown, specialty shops, and restaurants. The Waxhaw Historic District is listed on the National Register of Historic Places.

**Motto:** *"Proud of Our Past. Passionate About Our Future."*
