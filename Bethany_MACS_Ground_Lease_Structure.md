# The Solution: Ground Lease Structure

## Overview

Bethany Church keeps full ownership of their land. No selling required. A ground lease allows an investor to build on Bethany's property while protecting everyone's interests.

---

## Who Owns What

| Party | Owns |
|-------|------|
| Bethany Church | The land (forever) |
| Investor | The building (for lease term) |
| School | Operates in the building |

---

## How It Works

### 1. Bethany enters a ground lease with the investor
- Investor pays Bethany for the right to use the land
- Bethany keeps their land forever

### 2. Investor builds the school on Bethany's land
- Investor funds construction
- Building is investor's asset during the lease term

### 3. School pays rent to occupy the building
- Rent covers investor's costs + profit
- Rent can flow through Bethany or directly to investor

### 4. End of Lease
- Contract specifies that building transfers to Bethany
- Bethany owns the school without ever paying to build it

---

## Why Everyone Wins

| Party | Wins By |
|-------|---------|
| Bethany Church | Keeps land, gets a school built, makes money from ground lease, owns building at end |
| Investor | Gets rental income, has building as collateral, clear ROI path |
| School | Gets a facility to operate in |
| Town | Controlled development with a known party who has "pull" to navigate approvals |

---

## Key Points

- No selling of Bethany's land
- No upfront cost to Bethany
- Investor gets collateral (building lien) — not ownership of land
- Everyone's interests are protected legally
- School runs, pays rent, Bethany collects rent from investor or passes through

---

*This is a standard real estate structure used in churches, schools, and non-profits across the country. It works.*
