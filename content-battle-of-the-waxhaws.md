# Battle of the Waxhaws — June 5-7, 2026

**Step Into 1780 Right Here in Waxhaw**

The Museum of the Waxhaws is hosting the 3rd Annual Battle of the Waxhaws American Revolution Reenactment & Living History Weekend — and it's bigger than ever.

**What's happening:**

- **Friday, June 5** — Reenactors march into Downtown Waxhaw at 7 PM. Meet them at the Community Corner Lot by the historic water tank.

- **Saturday, June 6** — 10 AM to 4 PM. Battle reenactments, living historians, colonial encampments, hands-on activities for kids.

- **Sunday, June 7** — 10 AM to 3 PM. Final day of the living history experience.

**Why it matters:**
This is the 250th anniversary of America's independence. Over 100 living historians and reenactors are traveling from across the country to be here.

**Tickets:**
- General admission (both days): Available at museumofthewaxhaws.org
- Family passes available
- Kids 4 and under: Free

**Parking:**
Waxhaw Elementary School, 1101 Old Providence Rd (short walk to museum)

---

**The history behind it:**

The Battle of the Waxhaws was a Revolutionary War skirmish that took place right here in our backyard. The Museum of the Waxhaws preserves this history — it's the birthplace region of Andrew Jackson, our 7th President.
