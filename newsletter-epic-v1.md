## 🎣 The Hook

**Welcome to Waxhaw. Now here's everything you need to know.**

You didn't just move here. You moved to *the* fastest-growing small town in the Charlotte region. In ten years, we doubled in size. That means half your neighbors have been here less than you have — and the other half have seen three new subdivisions pop up where there used to be a cow farm.

Either way? You're going to need this guide.

## 🗺️ The Newbie Survival Guide

*(Because Google doesn't know what we know)*

### First things first: You need a person.

Not a real person (though we've got plenty of those too). You need a **baker**, a **mechanic**, and a **coffee person**.

**The Baker**: Matthews Cafe. Been here forever. The biscuits are the kind your grandmother would lie about. Get the apple fritter. Thank me later.

**The Mechanic**: Cox Automotive on Rosewood (yes, really — they're old-school honest and won't upsell you on stuff you don't need).

**The Coffee Person**: Daylight Coffee in downtown Waxhaw. It's small. It gets crowded. But the cold brew is worth the wait and the owner actually remembers your order after visit three.

### Second: You need a ritual.

**Sunday morning**: Grab coffee from Daylight, drive to the Waxhaw Farmer's Market (yes, we have one, and yes, it's actually good), and walk the kids around. Bonus points if you let them pick out a vegetable they'll refuse to eat later. It's about the attempt.

**Friday night**: Date night. We'll get to that.

**Saturday afternoon**: You're going to need a backup plan for when the kids say "we're bored" at 2pm. Here's your list:
- **Crafty Kids** in Marvin — painting ceramics, no appointment needed, parental supervision optional (for real, they handle it)
- **Carmel Kitchen** — half-day camp for the kids, actual food for you
- **Lake Lloyd at Cane Creek Park** — fishing, playground, and enough space to wear them out

### Third: The holidays are coming.

**Memorial Day** (May 26): Cane Creek Park does a community cookout. It's not fancy. That's the point. Bring a side, stay for the fireworks.

**4th of July**: The parade on Providence Road is the highlight of the year. Get there by 9am, stake your claim on the curb, and bring lawn chairs. The fireworks launch from the high school field — any hill on the north side works if you don't want to fight the crowd.

**Back to School**: The Teacher Supply Store on Highway 16 does a backpack drive. We'll remind you. It's the one most people miss.

## 🍝 The Deep Dive: Date Night Worth Leaving the House For

*(This week's full article: [Read "Date Night in Waxhaw: Where to Take Her" on the blog])*

Real talk: You live in one of the best little food scenes in Union County and you don't even know it.

**The Splurge**: Kitchen | San José — yes, it's a "chain." No, I don't care. The rooftop bar alone is worth the drive from anywhere in the greater area, and the empanadas are stupid good.

**The Romantic Move**: The Mill — it's in the old textile mill downtown, they use local ingredients, and there's not a bad table in the house. Reservations required. I'm not joking. They book out two weeks.

**The Hidden Gem**: Talley's BBQ. Don't let the gas station exterior fool you. This is the best smoked meat within 20 miles. Grab a plate, sit at the picnic tables, and pretend you're on a road trip even though you're 10 minutes from home.

**The "We Just Want Drinks" Spot**: The Waxhaw Brewing Company. Local beer, live music on weekends, and they're dog-friendly. If you're on a first date and you bring a dog? You're automatically interesting.

**The "Kids are with the sitter and we want to actually talk" Place**: 4000 Mexican Grill. It's loud enough that no one's listening, the margaritas are strong, and the Guac is made fresh at your table.

## 🌍 The Cause: Why This Matters

*(Yes, we're going to talk about this every week. Yes, it matters.)*

Here's the thing about local: it's not nostalgic. It's not "oh, remember when everything was small." It's *economic*. Every dollar you spend at a local business stays in this community — it pays the wages of your neighbor, it funds the PTO for the teacher who lives down the street, it keeps the lights on at the body shop on Rosewood Road.

**That's the cause.**

But we went a step further.

Every business on NearBuy? Every listing, every offer, every ad? **It includes meals donated to families who need them.** Right here in Waxhaw and across the globe.

We call it Glocal: local heart, global impact.

We're not asking you to feel guilty. We're asking you to notice that when you choose local — *really* choose it — you're already making a difference. You just didn't have a receipt for it before.

We'll track the meals. We'll show you the impact. And you'll know that supporting the businesses in this guide is also feeding families.

That's not charity. That's just... math.
