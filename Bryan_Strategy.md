## Biggest Risks & What to Avoid

1. **Trying to do too much at once** — Focus on ONE product first. Don't launch 3 ideas simultaneously.

2. **Perfectionism killing momentum** — "Good enough to sell" beats "perfect and never launched." Your first budget planner will be rough. Launch anyway.

3. **Not validating with real sales** — Ideas are worthless until someone pays. Every week without a sale is a week of no data.

4. **Expanding Squiggly Marketing** — This is a trap. More clients = more work = more dependent. Don't grow it. Extract cash from it.

5. **Building ReviewsNearMe before you have capital** — You need money for tools/website. Don't start until Budget Planners + Business-in-a-Box fund it.

6. **Ignoring the math** — If something isn't hitting revenue targets by Week 4, pivot. Don't cling to ideas that don't sell.

7. **Assuming you need a team** — You don't. Yet. But once revenue hits $5k/month, start budgeting for a VA to handle ReviewsNearMe delivery. That's how you go from working IN the business to working ON it.
